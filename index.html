Tokyo

Tokyo is a dazzling blend of ultra-modern skyscrapers and historic temples. From the bustling streets of Shibuya and the neon lights of Shinjuku to the peaceful gardens of the Imperial Palace, the city offers something for everyone. Wander through the traditional markets of Asakusa, where you can sample local street food and shop for handcrafted souvenirs, or take in the panoramic views from the observation deck of Tokyo Skytree. For a moment of tranquillity, visit the serene Meiji Shrine nestled within a lush forest. Whether you're a foodie eager to explore Michelin-starred restaurants and ramen stalls or a culture enthusiast fascinated by cutting-edge technology and ancient traditions, Tokyo promises an unforgettable experience at every turn.

Kyoto

Kyoto is Japan’s cultural heart, famous for its ancient temples, serene Zen gardens, and traditional tea houses. Stroll through the enchanting bamboo forest in Arashiyama or catch a glimpse of a geisha in the historic Gion district. Explore the majestic Kinkaku-ji, the Golden Pavilion, as it glistens in the sunlight, reflected in the surrounding pond. Wander the stone pathways of Higashiyama, lined with centuries-old wooden buildings, where you can enjoy matcha sweets and browse handcrafted pottery. In spring, marvel at the cherry blossoms in full bloom along the Philosopher’s Path, while autumn brings a fiery display of red and gold leaves to the temple gardens. Kyoto’s timeless charm offers a peaceful retreat from the modern world, inviting visitors to step back in time and immerse themselves in Japan’s rich heritage.

Mt.Fuji

Mount Fuji, Japan’s tallest and most iconic mountain, is a must-see for visitors. Whether you admire its perfect cone from afar or climb to its summit, Fuji’s beauty and spiritual significance make it a symbol of Japan. During the climbing season in summer, thousands of hikers ascend its trails to witness the breathtaking sunrise from the peak, a phenomenon known as Goraiko. Surrounding the mountain, the Fuji Five Lakes region offers stunning views, hot springs, and traditional ryokan inns for those seeking relaxation. In autumn, the area bursts into colour as vibrant foliage frames the mountain’s snow-capped peak. On clear days, Mount Fuji can be seen from Tokyo and even Yokohama, its majestic presence inspiring artists and poets for centuries. Whether you seek adventure, serenity, or spiritual reflection, Mount Fuji provides an unforgettable experience.

Must-Sees:
  • Pagoda for the perfect Fuji view
  • Fuji Five Lakes (Lake Kawaguchi is the most popular)
  • Hakone – famous for hot springs and Fuji views
  • Oshino Hakkai – traditional village with clear spring water ponds
  • Climbing the Yoshida Trail in the summer months
